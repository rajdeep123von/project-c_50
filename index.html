Camel Origami

Fun facts about Camel:

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Chameleon Origami

Fun facts about Chameleon:

  • Almost half of the world’s chameleon species live on the island of Madagascar.
  • Chameleon eyes have a 360-degree arc of vision and can see two directions at once.
  • Chameleons can see in both visible and ultraviolet light.
  • A chameleon's tongue is twice the length of its body.
  • When hunting food, the chameleon’s tongue can go as fast as 95 kilometers per hour!

Pigeon Origami

Fun facts about Pigeon:

  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years.
  • Although pigeon droppings are seen by some as a problem in modern society, a few centuries ago pigeon guano was seen as extremely valuable.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.
  • Pigeons can find their way back to the nest from 1300 mies away.

Teddy-Bear Origami

Fun facts about Bear:

  • Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees.
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  • Canada is home to nearly 60 % of the world’s polar bears.
  • The Asiatic black bear has the largest ears than other species of bears.

Panda Origami

Fun facts about Panda:

  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
  • Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.
  • Pandas like to lick copper and iron. Strange behavior!

Flying Cicada Origami

Fun facts about Flying Cicada:

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  • Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.
